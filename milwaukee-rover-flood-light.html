Milwaukee M18 ROVER Dual Power Flood Light Review: Stop Working in Your Own Shadow

By Marcus Reid

Half the mistakes I've seen made on a job happened because a guy couldn't actually see what he was doing. A clamp light on an extension cord helps until you trip over the cord. The Milwaukee M18 ROVER Dual Power flood light (model 2366) fixes both problems at once.

The number that matters: 4,000 lumens of clean, white output. That's not a flashlight — that's enough to light a whole work area, a garage bay, or the back of a job trailer. The head tilts through 120 degrees, so you aim the light where the work is instead of bouncing it off a ceiling and hoping.

The "dual power" name is the smart part. Drop an M18 battery in it and it's cordless — run it up to about 12 hours on an XC5.0 pack on the lower setting. But it also has an AC port: plug in an extension cord and it runs all day without touching a battery. Cordless when you're moving around, corded when you're parked in one spot. You're never stuck.

It carries an IP54 rating, meaning it shrugs off jobsite dust and a rain shower, and the housing is built to survive getting knocked off a bench — which it will be.

The honest downside is weight and battery appetite on the bright setting; run it wide open on a battery and you'll drain a pack. That's exactly why the AC cord option earns its keep for long jobs.

If you already run M18 tools, buy the bare light and use the batteries you've got. Seeing the work clearly is one of the cheapest upgrades to the quality of everything you build.
