Knipex Cobra Water Pump Pliers Review: The Grip That Doesn't Slip

By Marcus Reid

I resisted spending "German pliers money" for years. Then I borrowed a set of Knipex Cobras on a plumbing job, rounded off exactly zero fittings all day, and ordered my own that night. Some tools are hype. These are the real thing.

Quick verdict: If you do plumbing, mechanical, or any work where you grab round things and turn them, buy them. The price stings once; the performance pays you back every job. If you only tighten a garden hose now and then, cheaper pliers are fine.

What's genuinely good. The push-button adjustment is the whole story — one thumb press and the jaw slides to size, then locks so it won't creep open under load. That self-locking bite means you clamp a nut or a pipe and it stays clamped instead of slipping and chewing up the corners. The steel holds an edge on the teeth far longer than the box-store pliers I used to burn through, and the slim head gets into tight spots where fat pliers won't fit.

The catch — be honest with yourself. They cost two to three times what a no-name pair does. And because the teeth bite hard, they'll mark a chrome or soft-brass finish — wrap the jaws with tape or use the smooth-jaw version on show fittings.

Bottom line. This is the tool that stops you rounding off fittings and cursing. Buy the right size for your work — the 10-inch is the do-everything pick — and it's the last pair of adjustable pliers you'll buy for a long, long time.
