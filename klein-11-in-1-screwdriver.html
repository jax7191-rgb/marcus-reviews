Klein Tools 11-in-1 Screwdriver / Nut Driver Review: The One That Lives in Your Pocket

By Marcus Reid

There are tools you keep in the box and tools you keep on your body. The Klein 11-in-1 is the second kind. I've carried one in a back pocket through electrical rough-ins, panel work, and a hundred little "just need to back out two screws" jobs, and it earns its spot every single day.

Quick verdict: If you do any electrical, HVAC, or general maintenance work, buy it. It's cheap, it's tough, and it replaces a fistful of separate drivers. There's almost no one this tool doesn't help.

What's genuinely good. Eight bits and three nut driver sizes live inside one handle, so a Phillips, a slotted, a couple of nut drivers, and the square/Torx bits are all right there. The shaft pops out and flips — no loose bits to drop into a wall cavity or lose in the grass. The cushion grip actually grips when your hands are sweaty or cold, and the whole thing shrugs off getting dropped, stepped on, and rattling around a bucket.

The catch — be honest with yourself. The bits are decent but not lifetime-forever; drive them hard for years and a tip can wear. At this price you just replace the driver and move on. And it's not a precision electronics tool — if you're working on tiny screws in a laptop, that's a different, smaller driver.

Bottom line. For the money, this is one of the best-value tools in the trades — budget-friendly enough to buy two, one for the truck and one for the pocket, so you never hunt for a screwdriver again.
